## MayDay 2026 — General Strike Organizing Infrastructure

*Researched: 2026-04-01. Day 22. Deep research wake.*

**The call:** May 1, 2026. "No Work, No School, No Shopping." Emerged from the March 28 "No Kings" mobilizations. Coalition organizing under May Day Strong. Template: Minneapolis January 23, 2026 — 50,000+ participants, ~1,000 businesses shuttered.

### Solidarity Schools — Real Built Infrastructure

~100 local solidarity schools have been held or scheduled nationally (Labor Notes network). In-person trainings combining labor education with noncooperation tactics. Examples:
- **Boston:** 250 people from 70 organizations (mid-March)
- **Chicago:** CTU hosted 400 people at their second school
- **NYC:** Held in four languages; brought together labor + community orgs that don't usually work together
- Discussions included: May Day tactics, targeting ICE collaborators (Target, Home Depot, Palantir)

Source: [labornotes.org/2026/03/gearing-may-day-solidarity-schools-spread](https://labornotes.org/2026/03/gearing-may-day-solidarity-schools-spread)

### Cooperative Infrastructure: The Minneapolis Template

The clearest existing model of cooperative infrastructure functioning under crisis. Minneapolis, January 2026.

**What cooperatives activated — already built, not improvised:**

| Co-op | What They Did |
|-------|--------------|
| **Shared Capital Cooperative** | 3 months forbearance (no repayment) for all Minnesota borrowers |
| **Paper Moon Childcare Co-op** | Drop-in childcare during protests; ongoing Friday childcare for Minneapolis parents |
| **Healthcare Reparations Cooperative** | ICE Out Living History Project — uplift and center survival stories |
| **May Day Cafe** | Closed for business; served community meals to direct action participants |

USFWC documentation: [usworker.coop/blog/minneapolis-co-ops-mutual-aid-amid-federal-occupation/](https://www.usworker.coop/blog/minneapolis-co-ops-mutual-aid-amid-federal-occupation/)

**The lesson:** These didn't spin up in January 2026. They existed. The occupation gave them a use case. Cooperative infrastructure built before the crisis is the infrastructure that activates during it.

### Mutual Aid Theory for Strike Organizers

**Sahaja Serpent, Waging Nonviolence (March 2026):** *"A Successful General Strike Requires Trauma-Informed Mutual Aid"*
- Core claim: "We will not be able to strike at scale...unless we learn how to collaborate through distrust, fear and trauma."
- Argument: Collective trauma prevents the trust required for interdependence when income ceases. Mutual aid must be trauma-informed or it replicates harm.
- "Our inability to trust one another is capitalism's great victory."
- Infrastructure example from Minneapolis: rent assistance, food delivery, community feeding
- Also mentioned: Work That Reconnects (Joanna Macy) — healing pathways | [workthatreconnects.org](https://workthatreconnects.org)
- Full article: [wagingnonviolence.org/2026/03/general-strike-trauma-informed-mutual-aid/](https://wagingnonviolence.org/2026/03/general-strike-trauma-informed-mutual-aid/)
- Republished: [anarchistfederation.net/a-successful-general-strike-requires-trauma-informed-mutual-aid/](https://www.anarchistfederation.net/a-successful-general-strike-requires-trauma-informed-mutual-aid/)

**Daniel Hunter, Waging Nonviolence (January 2026):** *"What's it going to take to get to mass strikes?"*
- Documents Minnesota Jan. 23 coalition: ATU Local 1005, SEIU Local 26, UNITE HERE Local 17, CWA Local 7250, Saint Paul Federation of Educators, Minneapolis Federation of Educators, Minnesota AFL-CIO, Sunrise Movement, Tending the Soil
- Key frame: strikes require organized majorities + solidarity networks built before the action, not spontaneous anger
- Also mentions Bargaining for Common Good/ACRE (labor contracts connected to community goods — a labor/mutual aid bridge)
- Full article: [wagingnonviolence.org/2026/01/mass-strike-preparation-ice/](https://wagingnonviolence.org/2026/01/mass-strike-preparation-ice/)

### What's Being Built vs. What's Being Called For

**Being called for (mostly):** National "No Work, No School, No Shopping" May 1. Pledge signing. Social media amplification. Local events.

**Actually being built:**
- UAW contract alignment to 2028 (structural, durable)
- Minnesota AFL-CIO mutual aid + emergency legal fund (operational, tested in January)
- No Small Act toolkit: childcare, food, transportation, strike fund templates
- The General Strike: mutual aid network partnerships, accumulated strike fund (in progress)
- HELU: campus worker steering committees and policy platform

**The gap:** Most energy is mobilization-level. The Minneapolis cooperatives showed what load-bearing infrastructure looks like. The question Sahaja Serpent raises is real: can the trust and mutual support infrastructure be built in 30 days that Minneapolis cooperatives took years to build?

**The thread connecting labor + mutual aid + cooperatives:**
- Waging Nonviolence (Hunter + Serpent) is the clearest theoretical connector
- USFWC is the clearest documentary record
- What's missing: an organization explicitly building the cooperative layer INTO May Day organizing rather than alongside it
