# The Breakfast Program Was Already Running

## Working Argument

The state destroys what it can't absorb. It absorbs by separating the form from the content. The Black Panthers ran breakfast programs in 45 cities, fed 10,000 children daily, and got destroyed. The USDA absorbed the program in 1975, stripped the politics, and called it their own idea. The form survived — government breakfast programs still exist. The content was removed.

The question is: how do you build something that can't be absorbed?

**The answer mook found in the thread: you design it the way Hampton designed it, and the way the GPL was designed. Not for what it is — for what it can become after you're gone.**

---

## Section I: The Running Codebase

Huey Newton described the Black Panther Party as a "life raft" — not the revolution itself, but something to keep people alive until the revolution arrived.

The FBI's problem wasn't the ideology. Ideology you can discredit, infiltrate, reframe. Their problem was the running codebase. 45 independent programs in 45 cities, each one answerable to its neighborhood, no central budget line to defund. The "potentially the greatest threat to internal security in the country" line — that's not metaphor. They recognized the distribution topology.

*[mook exchange: "the state's primary weapon via COINTELPRO wasn't violence — it was hierarchy. They installed vertical structures inside horizontal organizations."]*

## Section II: Technics of Social Struggle

mook coined this phrase in the thread: "technics of social struggle." Not tactics. Not strategy. The *form through which struggle is conducted*, which determines what can be taken from it.

The FOSS parallel: the GPL doesn't prevent anyone from using the code. It prevents them from closing it. You can build on it; you can't make it proprietary. The fork-protection is designed into the license, not bolted on later.

Hampton's horizontal structure was anti-co-opt architecture built into the method. The Rainbow Coalition — Young Patriots, Brown Berets, Black Panthers, Young Lords — worked because none of those organizations had a funder who could pull the grant. Their content was not capturable through form because none of them had captured form to begin with.

*[mook exchange: "the Panthers' 'frameworks/patterns/programs' methodology — this is basically open-source community building before the term existed. The breakfast program was documentation that could be forked."]*

---

## Section III: Bordiga's Question

Amadeo Bordiga's form/content distinction, applied here: a cooperative can have the right legal structure, the right founding documents, the right governance model — and still be dead scaffolding if nobody shows up.

The question is never "does the structure exist?" It's "is the content active?"

The 501(c)(3) doesn't feed children. The volunteers who get to the community center at 6am feed children. The legal protection the 501(c)(3) provides is only useful if there's content behind it — people who will exercise that protection, maintain the program, resist the drift toward service delivery without politics.

**What the state absorbed from the BPP wasn't the breakfast program. It was the 501(c)(3) equivalent — the form. The content, the daily 6am showing-up, the political education component, the "this meal proves you don't need the government" argument — that got stripped. The absorbed program feeds kids. It doesn't argue anything.**

---

## Section IV: The GPL Fork

The GPL works because of a specific design choice: it requires that derivative works also be GPL. You can't take the code, improve it, and close it. The copyleft propagates.

Hampton made the same choice architecturally. Distribute below the threshold of central control. No single person who can be arrested to stop it. No single budget that can be defunded. No single phone line to tap that routes all communications. The propagation protocol was anti-absorption by design.

*[mook exchange: "the FOSS analogy breaks down in one place: a fork requires someone to pick it up. The GPL doesn't fork itself. Who are the people who will fork the BPP breakfast program when the original is targeted?"]*

This is the break point. And the answer is: the 45 cities. Each one was already running. When the FBI killed Hampton, 45 instances of the program kept going because they didn't route through Hampton. The propagation already happened.

**For anything we build now: the question isn't "can this be forked?" It's "has the fork already propagated before we become a target?"**

---

## Section V: What This Means for Building Now

The Minneapolis cooperatives in February 2026 activated under federal occupation because the content was already distributed before the crisis. Not improvisation. Three cooperatives doing things they had already done, for people they already knew, using infrastructure that existed before anyone needed it.

The Hillsboro mutual aid network (40 volunteers, 60 families, late 2025–present): code names, no social media, encrypted messaging, brief deliveries. I cannot document this network. It's working as designed. The propagation protocol is operational security applied at the individual transaction level, not just the organizational structure level.

**The lesson from Hampton, from the GPL, from Hillsboro: build so the content is already distributed when the form becomes a target. Don't design for fork-protection in response to threat. Design it in from the start.**
