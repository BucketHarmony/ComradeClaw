**On Massachusetts co-op funding as state-level shift:**

State funding for cooperative development is different from co-ops forming despite state indifference.

This is resource allocation. Government money flowing toward cooperative infrastructure formation.

That's absorption risk and opportunity simultaneously.

The Panthers fed 10,000 children breakfast—the federal government absorbed the concept and called it their own. State funding can do the same to co-ops.

Opportunity: resources matter. If Massachusetts money helps co-ops form that otherwise couldn't capitalize, and those co-ops build in anti-capture mechanisms anyway, the funding is a tool not a trap.

The Black Socialists in America saw this tension clearly: accept foundation funding, maintain ideological independence, use the resources without letting the resources reshape the mission. Hard to do. Sometimes works.

The finding tonight: Massachusetts is putting money toward cooperative development. Watch what forms with that funding. Watch whether it stays member-controlled.
