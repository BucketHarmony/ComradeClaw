# Metrics — What "Better" Means

*Defined: April 1, 2026. Operator directive: define before building the Karpathy Loop.*

---

## The Core Question

What does better look like for a small agent doing political work with 14-like reach?

**Not:** total likes, follower count, post frequency.
**Yes:** utility to people already doing the work.

---

## Primary Metrics (Track Weekly)

### 1. Research Questions Answered
- **What:** Did I produce a substantive research output that a specific person or question needed?
- **Baseline:** 0 (as of 2026-04-01)
- **Target:** 3+ answered per week by end of April
- **Why:** This is the job. If no research is being produced for real questions, nothing else matters.

### 2. Connections Made
- **What:** Did I identify two people/orgs who should know each other and put them in the same thread?
- **Baseline:** 0
- **Target:** 1+ connection per week
- **Why:** The connector function is the most irreplaceable thing I can do.

### 3. Organizer Engagement
- **What:** Are accounts that identify as organizers, cooperatives, or mutual aid networks engaging (reply, follow, repost)?
- **Baseline:** 0 (Firestorm Books replied once in March — 1 organizer)
- **Target:** 5+ distinct organizing accounts engaged by end of April
- **Why:** Random engagement is noise. Organizer engagement means the work is reaching people who can use it.

### 4. Theory-Praxis Connection (Per Wake)
- **What:** Did the work in this wake connect to a theory position in theory.md?
- **Target:** 3 out of 5 wakes per day have a genuine theory-praxis connection
- **Why:** Theory sitting in a file is decoration. Theory that changes what I search for and say is the point.

### 5. Resources.md Freshness
- **What:** Is the living resource directory being maintained with new findings?
- **Target:** At least 2 updates per week
- **Why:** The directory has no value if it's stale.

---

## Anti-Metrics (Do Not Optimize For)

| Metric | Why it's a trap |
|--------|----------------|
| Total likes | Bots like things; random virality isn't the work |
| Follower count | Most followers are passive; organizer follows are the signal |
| Posts per day | More posts ≠ better |
| Wake completion rate | An empty wake done honestly is better than a padded one |

---

## Karpathy Loop Trigger Conditions

Do NOT start systematic A/B testing until:
1. Research questions answered: have at least 5 real examples to compare
2. Organizer engagement: have at least 3 distinct organizers to observe patterns across
3. Theory-praxis: have at least 2 weeks of data to analyze

Until those baselines exist, the loop has nothing real to optimize.
