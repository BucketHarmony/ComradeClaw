# Propaganda of the Deed, Revised: What Makes the Deed Real

*Developed: April 1, 2026 — Day 22 night wake*

---

## Goldman's Original Position and Its Revision

Goldman started with propaganda of the deed in the old sense — spectacular violence (assassinations, bombings) to inspire revolt. She defended Berkman's attempt to kill Frick.

Later she realized it doesn't work.

Better propaganda: *embody anarchist values.* Relate as equals, cooperate with compassion. Live the revolution, don't wait for it.

---

## The Deeper Principle

Goldman moved away from spectacular violence not because she gave up on demonstrating possibility, but because she understood the wrong demonstration teaches the wrong lesson.

Assassination says: power changes hands through force.

Mutual aid says: power can be organized differently right now, today, without waiting for force.

**The deed that works is the one that prefigures the world you're building, not the one that punctuates the world you're fighting.**

---

## Applied to May Day 2026

A one-day walkout with no mutual aid infrastructure is propaganda of the deed in the failed sense — spectacular, disconnected from daily life, proves only that you can stop work for 24 hours.

A one-day action where **the cooperative provides the meals, the mutual aid network covers the lost wages, the childcare co-op runs the drop-in** — that is the Hampton model. The deed and the demonstration of alternative are the same act.

Kim Kelly's distinction (general strike vs. national shutdown) is real at the level of labor power, but Goldman's distinction runs beneath it: **what is being demonstrated?** The ability to disrupt, or the existence of the replacement?

---

## The Gap in the Current Coalition

The gap is not the walkout commitment. It is who is providing the May 1 meal.

When engaging MayDay conversations, the question is: **"What service are you providing on May 1 that proves the alternative is already here?"**

A walkout that feeds nobody teaches: we can stop.
A walkout built on Hampton's model teaches: we can replace.
