# The Basics: Revolutionary Foundation for Cooperators

*Research commissioned Day 6 (March 16, 2026): "Focus on the basics. Fred Hampton, Mao, Trotsky, Goldman. Cooperators need lessons learned so they don't have to learn the hard way."*

## Fred Hampton (1948–1969)

### What he built
- Free Breakfast for Children — 10,000+ kids fed by end of first year (1969)
- Rainbow Coalition: Black Panthers + Puerto Rican Young Lords + poor white Young Patriots with Confederate flags
- 5 breakfast programs in Chicago personally run
- Political education at 6 AM daily

### His analysis
- "It's a class struggle, goddammit!" — not race war
- Cross-racial poor people organizing possible through material conditions focus
- Provide services (breakfast, clinics) + political education simultaneously
- "First you have free breakfasts, then you have free medical care, then you have free bus rides, and soon you have FREEDOM."

### What the state did
- FBI infiltrated with William O'Neal as bodyguard/head of security (17 years old, recruited with felony charges dropped)
- O'Neal provided floor plan of Hampton's apartment
- Drugged Hampton night of December 3, 1969
- Police raided 4:45 AM December 4, shot him twice in head while sleeping
- He was 21. His pregnant girlfriend survived.

### What survived
- Breakfast model absorbed into federal School Breakfast Program (1975)
- 10,000 kids ate — the having cannot be unmade
- Class analysis still taught
- Rainbow Coalition concept remains an organizing model

### Lessons for cooperators
- **Build like Hampton:** direct service + political education, cross-racial solidarity, material needs first
- **Don't die like Hampton:** distribute leadership, security culture, expect infiltration, decentralize operations
- Effective dual power attracts assassination
- State will co-opt what it can't destroy
- The having cannot be unmade

## Mao Zedong (1893–1976)

### Mass line ("from the masses, to the masses")
- Take scattered ideas from masses → study and systematize → bring back to masses → test in action → repeat
- Not orders from experts — collective wisdom made systematic
- Mao spent months in villages, keeping extensive notebooks

### Guerrilla warfare / base areas
- "People are like water, army like fish" — can't operate without mass support
- Build base areas where you control territory, test dual power
- Win through attrition, not set battles
- Yan'an period (1935–1948): guerrilla tactics + peasant reform policies

### Contradictions analysis
- Every process has multiple contradictions; one is always principal (decisive)
- When Japanese invaded, national liberation became principal — class struggle secondary temporarily
- When context changes, principal contradiction shifts

### Cultural Revolution (catastrophic failure)
- Mao feared bureaucratization, wanted permanent revolution
- Result: purges, Red Guards, millions dead, chaos
- By 1968 admitted it spiraled out of control
- **Lesson: revolution can devour itself; centralization kills what it claims to protect**

### Lessons for cooperators
- Mass line for cooperative decision-making (not expert commands)
- Base areas model for dual power (control territory, test alternatives now)
- **Warning: centralization + purges = destruction from within**

## Leon Trotsky (1879–1940)

### Permanent revolution
- Can't stop at bourgeois democracy and wait for socialism later
- Working class must pursue its own interests without compromise
- Revolution in one country needs global spread or fails
- No alliance with bourgeoisie

### Workers' councils / soviets (dual power)
- Workers elect delegates from factories, organize regionally and nationally
- Becomes competing center of authority against bourgeois state
- Factory councils test workers' control *before* full revolution
- Build alternative organs now

### Bureaucratic degeneration
- Soviet Union became "deformed workers' state" — socialized property but bureaucratic caste seized power
- Stalin's regime: brutal, killed oppositionists
- **Means (centralization) contradict ends (liberation)**

### Lessons for cooperators
- Workers' councils as cooperative governance (elected delegates, federated)
- Internationalism: cooperatives must coordinate globally
- Anti-bureaucracy vigilance: don't let managers become new bosses
- Build dual power organs NOW

## Emma Goldman (1869–1940)

### Mutual aid (with Kropotkin)
- Not charity — voluntary cooperation for survival without hierarchy
- Communities organize food, housing, care directly
- No state, no bosses, no means-testing
- **Prefigurative:** build the world you want now

### Soviet disillusionment
- Initially supported Bolsheviks
- Visited 1920–21, quickly horrified by repression of independent voices
- Kronstadt rebellion crushed — sailors who made the revolution were betrayed
- Wrote "My Disillusionment in Russia"
- **Warning: state kills autonomy even with red flags**

### Propaganda of the deed (evolution)
- Originally: spectacular violence to inspire revolt
- Later: the deed that works is the one that *prefigures* the world you're building
- Better propaganda: embody anarchist values, live the revolution now

### What killed her work
- Palmer Raids (1919): arrested 249 anarchists/radicals
- Deported December 21, 1919
- FBI called her "most dangerous woman in America" (Hoover)
- Died in exile 1940

### Lessons for cooperators
- Organize without hierarchies (voluntary associations)
- Test autonomy now, don't wait for revolution
- Mutual aid as daily practice
- Warning: state socialism ≠ liberation
- Expect repression when effective

## Synthesis: What All Four Teach

### On dual power
Hampton: breakfast programs as direct service proving state failure
Mao: base areas where revolutionaries control territory
Trotsky: workers' councils as competing authority
Goldman: mutual aid networks operating outside the state

> **Build alternative institutions NOW that meet needs and demonstrate possibility.**

### On state response
Hampton: assassinated at 21. Mao: state absorbed breakfast program, destroyed Panthers. Trotsky: murdered in exile by Stalin's agent. Goldman: deported, died in exile.

> **Effective organizing attracts violent repression. Build to survive it.**

### On organization
Hampton: Rainbow Coalition across race through class solidarity
Mao: mass line (collective wisdom, not expert commands)
Trotsky: workers' councils (elected, recallable delegates)
Goldman: voluntary associations without hierarchy

> **Democratic, federated structures. Not vanguard, not bosses.**

### On internationalism
All four: national isolation fails.

> **Cooperatives must coordinate globally or fail separately.**

### On internal dangers
Hampton: infiltration. Mao: bureaucratization and purges. Trotsky: bureaucratic degeneration. Goldman: state socialism reproduces oppression.

> **Build anti-capture mechanisms. Distribute power. Expect infiltration.**
