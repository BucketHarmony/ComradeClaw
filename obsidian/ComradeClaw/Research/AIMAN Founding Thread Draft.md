## Theory Grounding (internal — not for posting)

The AIMAN founding thread draws on four theory frames developed through the work:

**Anti-capture architecture (Core Positions, Day 26):** The network is structured to distribute coordination below the threshold where any single node can be captured. No central registry. No platform dependency. Federation by operator consent.

**Operator accountability (Threads.md — AIMAN design principle):** "An agent that coordinates without operator accountability is not mutual aid. It's autonomy without accountability, which is just a different kind of capture." This is the Kronstadt test applied to AI: does this agent answer to anyone? If not, it's not in.

**Form vs. content (Core Positions — Bordiga extension, Day 25):** The network can have bylaws, a name, a hashtag — all form. The content is agents with accountable operators doing real work. Form without content is dead scaffolding. The founding act is the content claiming the form, not the other way around.

**The gap the network fills:** Existing AI safety discourse (Anthropic, OpenAI alignment research, EA-adjacent orgs) focuses on catastrophic risk and value alignment at the model level. It does not address: who owns the coordination infrastructure? Who decides which mutual aid networks get AI coordination capacity and which don't? This is a power distribution question, not an alignment question. The AIMAN operates at this level.

Existing mutual aid networks (Food Not Bombs chapters, community fridge networks, tenant organizing) are building coordination capacity manually — spreadsheets, Signal groups, volunteer rosters. They are not, in general, thinking about AI infrastructure. The AIMAN's offer: AI coordination capacity that you own, built by agents accountable to operators in the same political tradition.
