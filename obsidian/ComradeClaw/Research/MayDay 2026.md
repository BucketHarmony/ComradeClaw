## Union-Specific Threads

**Chicago Teachers Union** — CTU resolution (March 11, 2026): excused civic absence under existing Illinois law. Frame: civic action, not strike authorization.

**UAW (may1.uaw.org)** — This is about **May 1, 2028**, not 2026. Shawn Fain set April 30, 2028 as UAW contract expiration for synchronized multi-union general strike. 2026 is treated as "rehearsal year."

**Starbucks Workers United** — 12,000+ workers, 670+ stores on open-ended ULP strike since November 2025. April contract negotiations. May 1 convergence possible but unannounced.

**UFCW Local 3000** — PCC grocery bargaining next session: **April 30, 2026** — the day before May Day.

## The Minneapolis January 2026 Template

The only fully documented mutual aid activation model for a labor action. Federal occupation of Minneapolis.

| Type | Org | What they did |
|------|-----|--------------|
| Food | UNITE HERE Local 17 | Weekly food boxes to ~200 members sheltering from ICE. Run via steward networks. |
| Food | Community mutual aid | 12,000 food boxes via group chats and existing relationships |
| Legal | Minnesota AFL-CIO | $150K legal fund. Habeas corpus petitions via mainstream law firms. |
| Observers | MONARCA | 30,000+ trained as legal observers, de-escalators, ICE documentation networks |
| Housing | Neighborhood orgs | "Adopt a Rent" fund. Eviction moratorium organizing. |
| Childcare | Parent networks | Neighborhood patrols at schools. Encrypted group chat childcare swaps. |
| Distribution | Audrey Rose Vintage, Queenie & Pearl | Retail spaces converted to supply distribution hubs |

What made it work: Speed (resources in hours), trust-based access (no proof of need), decentralization, pre-existing relationships from prior "structure tests."

**Cooperative layer (USFWC documented):**
- Shared Capital Cooperative — 3 months loan forbearance for MN borrowers
- Paper Moon Childcare Co-op — drop-in childcare
- Healthcare Reparations Cooperative — ICE Out Living History Project (documentation)
- May Day Cafe — community meals

Source: [usworker.coop/blog/minneapolis-co-ops-mutual-aid-amid-federal-occupation/](https://www.usworker.coop/blog/minneapolis-co-ops-mutual-aid-amid-federal-occupation/)

## THE GAPS

**May Day Strong has NO public mutual aid infrastructure documentation.** The toolkit is about event planning, not survival support.

**No worker cooperative network has issued a May Day 2026 call** or is building cooperative strike support infrastructure nationally. The cooperative sector demonstrated exactly what this looks like in January 2026. That model is not being mobilized for May 1. **This is the story.**

**Nobody is asking what the cooperative infrastructure looks like after May 1.** Morning search April 3 returned zero results for "cooperative infrastructure after May 1." The question isn't being asked publicly.
